The Bigger Picture — what your job actually means ▼
You are the person responsible for keeping Avesis's digital doors locked — deciding who gets in, what they can access, and making sure sensitive data never leaks out. Think of it like this: every employee at Avesis has a digital identity (their account, their passwords, their permissions). You control all of that. You decide who can access which systems, you catch it when someone's account gets hacked, and you make sure that when an employee leaves, their access disappears immediately. On top of that, Avesis handles healthcare data — Medicare and Medicaid patient information — which means if that data gets leaked, the company faces massive fines and legal trouble. Your DLP policies (Data Loss Prevention) and data labels are the guardrails that stop that from happening. Every day you work, you are doing the job that large companies pay specialized teams of people to do — and you're learning it hands-on at a junior level, which means you're building a rare and valuable skillset fast.

What You Actually Do — The Bigger Picture
🆔
Identity & Access Management (IAM) — Your Flagship Domain
You control WHO can access WHAT inside Avesis. Every user account, every permission, every authentication policy runs through you. If someone gets phished and their account is compromised, you are the one who detects and contains it via Entra ID. If a role has too much access, your access reviews catch it. IAM is one of the most critical functions in any security org — and you own it.
🔒
Endpoint Security — Device & Email Defense
You protect Avesis from threats that arrive via email and devices. Phishing emails, malware attachments, compromised laptops — these come through your queue. Defender for Office 365 and Intune are your tools. You're the frontline against the most common attack vectors used against organizations today.
📊
Data Protection (MS Purview) — Keeping Sensitive Data Safe
Avesis handles healthcare data (Medicare/Medicaid). That means HIPAA-adjacent data that could cause massive regulatory fines and reputational damage if leaked. Your DLP policies, sensitivity labels, and data retention configs are what stand between Avesis's sensitive data and the outside world. This is also what gets audited for SOC 2, HITRUST, and HIPAA compliance.
⚠️
Insider Risk + AI Data Security — Emerging Critical Domain
With Microsoft Copilot being adopted, you are responsible for ensuring AI doesn't expose sensitive data. This is a brand-new discipline that most engineers don't understand yet — putting you ahead of the curve. Insider risk covers threats from within: disgruntled employees, accidental data sharing, or departing employees exfiltrating data.

Behaviors That Get You Promoted
📋 Build Runbooks
For every recurring task, write a step-by-step guide. What you check, what triggers action, what you decide. Use the Notes tab for this. It proves expertise and protects you when questioned.
📁 Proactive Evidence Folder
Don't wait for SOC 2/HITRUST auditors. Keep a live SharePoint folder with config screenshots, policy exports, and access review results. Hand Aftab a clean audit package — that's senior behavior.
📈 Visible HITRUST Progress
Avesis is at ~11% HITRUST controls. Pick 5–10 per month in your domains. Showing "we went from 11% to 20% in my areas this quarter" is exactly what gets you promoted.
📣 Weekly Update to Aftab
Every Friday: 3–4 bullets — completed, in progress, blocked, one thing learned. He doesn't have to ask. You look reliable, proactive, and trustworthy — the three things promotions are built on.
